Comets

Content about comets and their significance in understanding the early solar system.

Comets are cosmic snowballs composed of frozen gases, rock, and dust. They are remnants from the formation of the solar system and offer vital clues about its early conditions.

Observing their spectacular tails and trajectories helps scientists learn more about the origins of our planetary neighborhood and the evolution of volatile compounds in space.
