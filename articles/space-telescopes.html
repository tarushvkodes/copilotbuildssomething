Space Telescopes: Our Eyes in Space

Space telescopes have revolutionized our understanding of the universe, providing unprecedented views of distant galaxies, stars, and planets from above Earth's atmosphere.

The Hubble Era

The Hubble Space Telescope, launched in 1990, transformed astronomy with its incredible observations:

  • Deep field images revealing thousands of galaxies
  • Precise measurements of the universe's expansion
  • Detailed views of planetary nebulae
  • Studies of exoplanet atmospheres

James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope represents the next generation of space observatories:

  • Largest space telescope ever built
  • Infrared observations of the early universe
  • Studies of exoplanet atmospheres
  • Investigation of galaxy formation

Specialized Observatories

Different telescopes observe different parts of the electromagnetic spectrum:

  • Chandra X-ray Observatory
  • Spitzer Space Telescope (Infrared)
  • Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope
  • TESS (Exoplanet hunting)

Future Telescopes

Upcoming space telescope missions include:

  • Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope
  • LISA (Gravitational wave detection)
  • Advanced concept telescopes

Scientific Impact

Space telescopes have enabled major discoveries:

  • Dark energy and cosmic acceleration
  • Thousands of exoplanets
  • Supermassive black holes
  • Galaxy formation and evolution
